# Google Public Sources

Last updated: 2026-06-29

## Purpose

These public Google sources shape the roadmap, rubrics, and interview focus in this learning system.

## Core References

1. Google re:Work, Set goals with OKRs
   https://rework.withgoogle.com/intl/en/guides/set-goals-with-okrs
   Why it matters: monthly and quarterly reviews are built around measurable goals and grading.

2. Google re:Work, Developing great managers at Google
   https://rework.withgoogle.com/intl/en/guides/developing-great-managers-at-google
   Why it matters: the roadmap adapts Deliver Results, Develop People, and Build Community into an IC growth model.

3. Google re:Work, Understand team effectiveness
   https://rework.withgoogle.com/intl/en/guides/understand-team-effectiveness
   Why it matters: psychological safety, dependability, structure and clarity, meaning, and impact inform collaboration and review standards.

4. Google re:Work, Coaching for everyone
   https://rework.withgoogle.com/intl/en/guides/coaching-for-everyone-the-amplified-impact-of-peer-coaching
   Why it matters: monthly and quarterly growth loops include peer coaching and feedback capture.

5. Google Engineering Practices Documentation
   https://google.github.io/eng-practices/
   Why it matters: code health, review quality, and small-change discipline are built into weekly and monthly scoring.

6. Google Engineering Practices, The Standard of Code Review
   https://google.github.io/eng-practices/review/reviewer/standard.html
   Why it matters: review quality is based on continuous code-health improvement rather than perfectionism.

7. Google Engineering Practices, What to look for in a code review
   https://google.github.io/eng-practices/review/reviewer/looking-for.html
   Why it matters: design, functionality, complexity, tests, naming, style, consistency, and documentation shape review criteria.

8. Google Engineering Practices, Small CLs
   https://google.github.io/eng-practices/review/developer/small-cls.html
   Why it matters: weekly project scope is intentionally broken into reviewable, self-contained increments.

9. Google Testing Blog, Better Feedback Makes for Faster Reviews
   https://testing.googleblog.com/2023/08/testing-on-toilet-presents.html
   Why it matters: review quality and interview questions include respectful, specific, trade-off-aware feedback.

10. Google SRE Books
    https://sre.google/books/
    Why it matters: reliability, observability, release discipline, and operational excellence are core architect competencies.

11. Google SRE, Release Engineering
    https://sre.google/sre-book/release-engineering/
    Why it matters: monthly and quarterly production gates emphasize repeatable releases, canaries, and rollback readiness.

12. Google SRE Book Table of Contents
    https://sre.google/sre-book/introduction/
    Why it matters: SLOs, toil reduction, monitoring, alerting, incidents, postmortems, and testing for reliability are mapped into the second half of the year.

## Interpretation Note

This roadmap uses public Google material as an inspiration model for rigor, not as a claim that it reproduces Google’s private internal promotion ladder.
