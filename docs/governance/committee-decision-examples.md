# Committee Decision Examples

Last updated: 2026-06-29

## Purpose

These examples show how steering or promotion committee decisions should be written.

## Example 1: Continue as Planned

- committee: Steering Committee
- decision: continue as planned
- rationale:
  Atlas is moving every month, review closure is strong, and blockers are manageable.
- evidence cited:
  month scorecards, weekly reports, issue snapshots
- owner:
  learner
- follow-up action:
  keep current roadmap sequence
- next review date:
  end of next quarter

## Example 2: Increase Rigor

- committee: Steering Committee
- decision: increase rigor on validation evidence
- rationale:
  documentation quality is high, but reliability proof is thinner than expected.
- evidence cited:
  quarterly KPI sheet, evidence registry, replay pack
- owner:
  mentor
- follow-up action:
  require drill or trace evidence for every critical issue next month
- next review date:
  next monthly review

## Example 3: Delay Promotion

- committee: Promotion Committee
- decision: not ready yet
- rationale:
  architectural reasoning is credible, but system-wide reliability evidence is still incomplete.
- evidence cited:
  promotion packet, annual review memo, benchmark notes
- owner:
  reviewer panel
- follow-up action:
  add one quarter of stronger production-style evidence
- next review date:
  next promotion checkpoint
