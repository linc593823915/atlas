## Quarterly Assessment Standard

- Score out of 100.
- Deliver Results: 20 points.
- Technical Judgment: 20 points.
- Code Health and Simplicity: 15 points.
- Reliability and Operational Excellence: 15 points.
- Communication and Documentation: 15 points.
- Build Community and Learning Velocity: 15 points.
- Not Yet: below 80 or missing quarter-critical artifacts.
- Meets: 80-91 with complete evidence and defensible technical reasoning.
- Strong: 92+ with architect-level influence, trade-off clarity, and cross-cutting Atlas improvement.

## Review Format

- 30-minute self-review against the quarter objective and evidence checklist.
- 45-minute mock interview loop covering coding, design, reliability, and behavioral questions.
- 45-minute Atlas architecture walkthrough with trade-off discussion.
- Written retrospective with remediation items for the next quarter.

## Evidence Checklist

- All monthly OKRs are graded and explained.
- Atlas milestone for the quarter is demonstrable end to end.
- Tests, drills, traces, or benchmark evidence exist where relevant.
- Design, docs, and runbooks are current enough for another engineer to continue the work.
- At least one explicit memory update captures reusable decisions or conventions.

## Interview Questions

1. Design the end-to-end Atlas subsystem portfolio after Quarter 1: what are the core components, contracts, and state boundaries?
2. What was the hardest design trade-off in Quarter 1, and what evidence drove your decision?
3. Which subsystem from Quarter 1 would you put through a design review first, and what would you challenge?
4. How would you test the most important failure mode introduced in Quarter 1?
5. Where did code health improve, and where is the system still fragile?
6. If a new engineer joined now, what documentation would let them become productive quickly?
7. How did you use goals, grading, and retrospectives to keep the quarter measurable?
8. What would a staff-level engineer say is still missing after Quarter 1?
9. Tell me about a week where you intentionally chose progress over perfection. What guardrails did you keep?
10. Describe a case where you changed your mind because tests, traces, or measurements contradicted your intuition.
11. How did you create psychological safety or peer-coaching behavior in a self-study or team setting?
12. What evidence from Atlas proves that you can operate, not just build, agent systems?
